Career

Meisha Tate’s first amateur bout happened against Muay Thai specialist Elizabeth Posener in March 2006. Before turning pro, her amateur record was 5-1. In November 2007, she made her professional debut as a mixed martial artist.

In April 2009, Tate defeated Liz Carreiro to win the FCF Women’s Bantamweight Championship. She successfully defended her title in January 2010 against Valerie Coolbaugh.

In Strikeforce, she debuted in June 2008. In her debut match, she went on to defeat Elaina Maxwell. At the women’s tournament of Strikeforce in August 2010, Tate became the Strikeforce Women’s Bantamweight Tournament Champion by defeating Hitomi Akano.

She joined UFC in February 2013. In December 2013, she lost against Ronda Rousey for the UFC Women’s Bantamweight Championship. She became the UFC Bantamweight Champion in March 2016 by defeating Holly Holm. In November 2016, Tate lost to Raquel Pennington at the UFC 205. Following that, she announced her retirement from MMA.
