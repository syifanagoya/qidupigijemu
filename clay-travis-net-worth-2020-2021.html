Career

Clay Travis started his career as a lawyer in the U.S. Virgin Islands in 2004. At the same time, he got media attention with his personal blog. He is an ardent Tennessee Titans fan but was not able to get the satellite package to watch the game in his hometown, so he went on a “pudding strike,” eating just pudding for more than fifty days. His effort was not successful in forcing DirecTV to sell packages in his city. However, he blogged about his experience, which got several media attention.

In 2005, he was hired by CBS Sports to write online, but he was not paid for the first year. The following year he gave up his law practice and started working full-time for CBS. He visited more than 12 football stadiums and wrote about the experience in the book “Dixieland Delight.”

Later, Travis left CBS and joined the sports blog Deadspin, and also the sports website FanHouse as a national columnist. In 2011, he founded the site Outkickthecoverage.com which became one the largest and most visited college football websites on the internet.

In 2008, he collaborated with D1 Sports to prepare the NFL Draft and later wrote about the experience on a blog entitled Rough Draft. Travis was also the co-host of the radio talk show, 3HL, which aired on 104.5 The Zone. He was also the host of a national sports radio show on NBC Sports.

In 2014, Travis was hired by Fox Sports for its weekly college football Saturday pregame show. He hosts the daily television show on Periscope and Facebook.

As an undergrad, while he was still at George Washington University, Travis worked for the U.S. Representative Bob Clement as an intern for four years. In 2001, he was hired to work for Al Gore’s presidential campaign.
