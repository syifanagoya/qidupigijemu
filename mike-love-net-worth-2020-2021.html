Early Life

Mike Love was born on the 15th of March in the year 1941 in the Los Angeles megacity of California, to parents Emily and Edward Milton Love. Mike had attained education from the Dorsey High School from where he had graduated in the year 1959.

Being a part of the family that reputedly admired the creativity and had a glorious background in music, Mike had soon discovered his career. He would fan his voice during the family get-togethers, especially during Christmas. This had brought him to the attention of Brian Wilson. Henceforth, the duo went ahead to establish the renowned Beach Boys in the year 1961. The initiative was majorly addressed by the devotion of Brian towards Four Freshmen.

Career

During the initial days, Mike had stepped into the music world with his skills in saxophone contributing to the Beach Boys, in which he had soon gained a stable position with fellow artists Gary Usher, Roger Christian, and few others. He had emerged as a flourishing lyricist with “The Warmth Of The Sun,” composed in the year 1963.

The band, though started on a small scale, had soon gained immense popularity on a global scale. The band would specifically surround their theme based on the Southern California beach and surf songs, which had touched the hearts of innumerable people. In the year 1961, they had launched their first solo named “Surfin,” which had soon hit the 75th position among the top 100 Billboard’s List. The band had soon signed with Capitol Records in the year 1962. The year had brought them additional glory when their album entitled “Surfin’ Safari” had reached ranked 32 on the Billboard’s record.

Within a very few years, Mike had emerged as a lead vocalist, lyricist, and onstage glamour of the band. His contributions in “Little Deuce Coupe,” “Surfin’ U.S.A.,” “Be True To Your School,” “Fun Fun Fun,” “Good Vibrations” and “California Girls” had earned immense appreciation from his followers.

In the year 1988, Mike had also co-written the beautiful song “Kokomo.” Another excelling hit from the band counts upon the “That’s Why God Made The Radio.” Thereafter, the band crossed several records and continued to glorify their art and outreached to innumerable people all over the world.
